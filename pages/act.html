WHAT TO DO
with the knowledge you now have

There are too many contributors to depression to be numbered in full, but these are some of the most common sources of trauma:

  1. Loss of a loved one
  2. Loss of employment
  3. Constant abuse / fights
  4. Being bullied
  5. Feeling trapped

TREATMENT OPTIONS:

Therapy

Speaking with a trained professional is a phenominal way to express unspoken emotions, or release tension. It is the most difficult option, but yields the greatest results.

Because of the lack of trust towards other people, denial may contribute to their isolation. Accepting that there is a problem is the hardest first step, but openning themselves up will do the most immediate good. It is not the ultimate solution to every situation, but is the best place to start. Group therapy is also a wonderful option, as long as there is open communication.

Antidepressants

  1. Prozac (fluoxetine)
  2. Zoloft (sertraline)
  3. Lexapro (escitalopram)

CONSULT A DOCTOR BEFORE USING THIS

Not all depression issues need to be solved by medication. This is the common assumption that depressed people are "imbalanced" and need to take a pill to feel better. This midset is not the one to instill, as it can lead to substance abuse.

Exercise

Increases endorphins in the brain, which signal pleasure and satisfaction. The physical effort will provide motivation to improve their mindset, as well as their health.

This does not need to be a regiment that requires a gym membership, purchasing equiptment, or putting out a lot of money for supliments. This could be as simple as going for a walk, push-ups, swimming, or something that doesn't require outside help.

Meditation

It may only require 5 minutes a day, but sitting somewhere quietly without any distractions will go a long way into calming the mind, and improving the color of their thoughts.

Visual and audio stimulation are masks that they feel in private, to help them not focus on how they feel. By addressing their feelings and emotions in quiet introspection, they can allow their healthy behaviors to take root, instead of the negative ones.

Creative Outlets

There are more creative hobbies than can be expressed. Studies show that increased left-brain activity stimulates the entire brain.

Options include: Music, painting, reading, writing, sculpting, designing, drawing, singing, acting, animating, etc.

Depressed people can be some of the most beautifully expressive artists, because they are channeling their emotions into their craft. There may still be underlining issues to work out, but it is doing something with all the negative emotion that is key.
