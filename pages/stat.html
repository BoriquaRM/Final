STATISTICS / SYMPTOMS

THE AVERAGE U.S. POPULATION
affected by Depression

The average total population of the U.S. is 300 million

7.1% of the population is registered, or admitted, to be depressed. And this number is just the ones that are known of.

17.3 million people, from child to adult. Too many go through life without knowing that their grief is a mental illness, and one that can be treated.

9.1% of the number of depressed people are children from the ages of 8-17.

1.9 million children are enduring their schooling experience with a burden that they are not able to handle.

If these same numbers were to be applied to schools and workplaces around the country...

Schools with an average of
20 depressed children

95,000 schools

would be affected

Large businesses with an average of
100 depressed adults

308,000 businesses

would be affected

The ratio of depression among men and women
2 : 1

Reasons for this could be that childbirth includes port-pardom depression, in which the hormones involved with creating life are suddenly thrown out of balance, and the mother is left with a sense of loss.

The older generation is less represented

With a noticible drop in age representation, it is a clear indicator that depression and suicide likely have this common denominator, as the elderly cannot cope with the extreme levels of sadness and loss.

⚠ SYMPTOMS AND SIGNS ⚠
to be aware of in yourself and others

Empathy Confusion

It's difficult to understand what "happy people" feel, even if they considered themselves happy once.

  1. Confused when is a seemingly animated setting.
  2. Squinting and redirecting their gaze when there are loud noises.
  3. Trouble understanding social queues.

Masked Emotions

Will mask their emotions with faux-happiness, even to the point of overboard. It is a coping mechanism.

  1. Over-exaggerated/forced laughter.
  2. Hunched shoulders, as if standing up straight requires too much effort.
  3. Frowning as a normal expression.
  4. Smiling suddenly when people approach, to throw off suspicion.

Trust Issues

Will have a isolated bubble around them, prefering not to socialize with anyone around them.

  1. "Wall-flowers", or considered to be "loners".
  2. They only allow select individuals into their circle, because they are trusted.

Indecision

While many treatment options exist, there can be little difference from one over another, and all will be deemed useless.

  1. Frustration will eliminate viable options.
  2. Refusal to accept help from others.
  3. Chosing "easier" options, rather than proven ones.
